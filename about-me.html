About Me

Ever since I built my first computer and started researching the inner workings of a computer, I immediately fell in love with technology. The sheer complexity of all the systems and components that work together to make up our phones, tablets, IoT devices, and computers is genuinely awe-inspiring. Learning a new feature in a programming language, learning a new framework, or stumbling upon that Stack Overflow forum that finally provides a solution to that obscure error you've been stuck on for hours is what I live for!

I've been an avid PC gamer since I was a teenager, always preferring fantasy RPG games. I'm too busy these days to game much, but it was heavily modding Skyrim that initially sparked my interest in coding. Getting over 100 mods to work and resolving conflicts between mods was more fun to me than actually playing the game with all the mods working correctly. This eventually inspired me to purchase my first coding book, “Coding for Dummies,” as I wanted to develop a deeper understanding of the code so I could learn how to make my own mods one day. This book marks the beginning of my software developer journey, and shortly after I finished it I found myself enrolled in the DMIT program at NAIT, preparing for my first term by taking as many online coding courses as possible.

Having hobbies and a way to express myself creatively has always been important to me. Some of my fondest childhood memories were building model cars with my dad and sharing with him the satisfaction when you finally attach the last piece and add it to the display. Moving to Edmonton when my parents separated was one of the most challenging circumstances to adapt to, and although I continued this hobby for a while, I would soon discover a new creative outlet in music. Taking a guitar class in high school was life-changing for me, and I've played in bands to this day ever since. Although I'm no longer pursuing music as a career, playing guitar is still one of my favorite hobbies. The creative elements of software development and the satisfaction I experience when solving problems are what I find most appealing about it and why I am so eager to put my passion to work in the industry.

As a Northern Alberta Institute of Technology student, I am up to date with industry standards and best practices and inspired by industry leaders to produce the best, most efficient code I possibly can.
